The Variety of Golf in New Zealand

New Zealand is a country of contrasts, and this is reflected in the variety of golf experiences it offers. From coastal links to lush parklands, the diversity of courses across the country ensures that every golfer can find something that suits their taste and skill level.

Coastal Links and Seaside Gems

New Zealand’s extensive coastline is home to some of the most beautiful and challenging links courses in the world. These courses, often set against the backdrop of dramatic cliffs and roaring seas, offer a true test of skill, where the elements play a significant role in the game. Courses like Paraparaumu Beach on the Kapiti Coast and Chisholm Links in Dunedin are perfect examples of this style of golf, offering rugged beauty and a tough challenge for even the most seasoned players.

Mountain and Lakeside Courses

For those who prefer playing amidst mountains and lakes, New Zealand’s South Island offers an array of courses that combine golf with breathtaking scenery. Queenstown, known for its adventure tourism, also boasts some of the most spectacular golf courses in the country. Jack’s Point, with its rugged terrain and stunning views of Lake Wakatipu, is a must-play for any golfer visiting the region. Similarly, the Kinloch Club near Taupo offers a challenging course designed by Jack Nicklaus, set in a landscape of rolling hills and native bush.

Parkland Courses and City Escapes

In contrast to the rugged coastal and mountain courses, New Zealand also offers a selection of lush parkland courses, often located close to major cities. These courses provide a more traditional golfing experience, with manicured fairways, mature trees, and well-maintained greens. The Royal Wellington Golf Club, one of the oldest and most prestigious in the country, offers a classic parkland setting just outside the capital. Meanwhile, courses like the Auckland Golf Club provide a peaceful escape from the city, with beautifully designed holes set amidst tranquil surroundings.

Unique and Quirky Courses

New Zealand is also home to some unique and quirky golf experiences that you won’t find anywhere else. For example, the course at Wairakei Golf + Sanctuary near Taupo not only offers a top-quality golfing experience but also serves as a wildlife sanctuary, where players can spot native birds and animals as they make their way around the course. Another example is the Cape Kidnappers course, where sheep and cattle freely roam the fairways, adding a distinctive New Zealand touch to your round.

Something for Everyone

Whether you’re looking for a challenging test of skill or a relaxing round in beautiful surroundings, New Zealand’s variety of golf courses ensures that there’s something for everyone. The country’s unique landscapes, combined with a strong golfing tradition, make it a destination like no other for golf enthusiasts.
